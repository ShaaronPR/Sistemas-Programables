- **Introduction** 

The servos are a special type of D.C motor, that are characterized by their capacity of immediately positioning themselves in any position within their operating range between an interval of operation. To do this, the servo motor waits for a pulse of the train that corresponds with the movement to do. They are generally made up of an amplifier, a motor, a reductor system made by toothed wheels and a feedback circuit, everything packed in a box of little dimensions.

In this practice, servos are gone to be used to position elements of control like levers, small elevators and rudders. Also, they are used in radio-control, puppets and of course, robots. The servos are extremely useful in robotics.

The electric circuit is in charge of managing the movement and position of the motor.

The necessity of a control signal for the functioning of this type of motors makes it impossible for them to be used without  the adequate control circuit. This is because for the internal control circuit to work, it‚Äôs necessary a modulated control signal. For this is used pulse width modulation (PWM).

The electronic circuit is in charge of receiving the PWM signal and translating it to movement for the DC Motor. The axis of the DC motor it‚Äôs coupled to a potentiometer, which allows voltage division. The out voltage of the divisor varies according to the axis position in the DC motor.

The signals of PWM required for the electronic circuit are similar for the majority of servo models. This signal has a squared formed wave. Depending on the width of pulse, the motor will adopt a fixed position:
